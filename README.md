# Costco FP&A Operating Forecast & Scenario Analysis

This project presents a Costco FP&A operating forecast and scenario analysis model built in Microsoft Excel. The model includes historical financials, forecast assumptions, an operating forecast, scenario analysis, diluted EPS sensitivity analysis, dashboard charts, and written business summaries.

The purpose of this project is to demonstrate financial modeling, FP&A forecasting, scenario planning, sensitivity analysis, margin analysis, and Excel dashboard presentation skills.

## Repository Description

Costco FP&A model with FY2029 scenario analysis, sensitivity analysis, EPS forecast, revenue forecast, margin analysis, and dashboard charts.

## Project Overview

This model analyzes Costco’s historical financial performance and projects future operating results through FY2029. The forecast is driven by key assumptions including net sales growth, membership fee growth, merchandise costs as a percentage of net sales, SG&A as a percentage of total revenue, tax rate, interest expense, interest income, and diluted shares.

The model includes downside, base case, and upside scenarios to evaluate how changes in operating assumptions affect revenue, profitability, margins, and diluted EPS.

## Key Features

* Historical financial statement analysis from FY2021A to FY2025A
* Operating forecast from FY2026E to FY2029E
* Forecast assumptions for revenue growth, membership fees, merchandise costs, SG&A, tax rate, interest, and diluted shares

The operating forecast projects Costco’s revenue, gross profit, SG&A, EBIT, EBT, income taxes, net earnings, diluted shares, and diluted EPS through FY2029E.

In the base operating forecast, Costco’s total net revenue increases from $275.2B in FY2025A to approximately $387.8B in FY2029E. Diluted EPS increases from $18.21 in FY2025A to $23.41 in FY2029E.

## Scenario Analysis

The scenario analysis tests downside, base case, and upside operating assumptions for FY2029. The assumptions are adjusted across net sales growth, membership fee growth, merchandise costs as a percentage of net sales, SG&A as a percentage of total revenue, tax rate, and diluted shares.

![Scenario Analysis Part 1](./Scenario%20Analysis%20Part%201.png)

The FY2029 scenario output shows how Costco’s financial results change under each case. In the base case, Costco reaches total net revenue of approximately $388.4B and diluted EPS of $24.48. The downside case results in diluted EPS of $18.68, while the upside case results in diluted EPS of $33.08.

![Scenario Analysis Part 2](./Scenario%20Analysis%20Part%202.png)

## Sensitivity Analysis

The sensitivity analysis tests FY2029 diluted EPS based on changes in net sales growth and merchandise costs as a percentage of net sales.

In the sensitivity table, diluted EPS ranges from $22.01 at 6.50% net sales growth and 89.50% merchandise costs to $27.58 at 10.50% net sales growth and 88.70% merchandise costs.

The analysis shows that Costco’s EPS is more sensitive to changes in merchandise costs than net sales growth. Because Costco operates with thin margins, even small changes in merchandise costs can have a meaningful impact on profitability.

![Sensitivity Analysis](./Sensitivity%20Analysis%20.png)

## Dashboard Charts

The dashboard section includes charts for total net revenue, diluted EPS, EBIT vs. net earnings, and margin comparison by scenario.

Costco’s FY2029 total net revenue increases across the three scenarios, from $354.1B in the downside case to $388.4B in the base case and $447.4B in the upside case. The increase is mainly driven by stronger net sales growth and higher membership fee growth in the upside scenario.

Costco’s FY2029 diluted EPS ranges from $18.68 in the downside case to $24.48 in the base case and $33.08 in the upside case. This shows that earnings per share are highly sensitive to changes in revenue growth, merchandise costs, SG&A expenses, and tax assumptions.

![Graphs Part 1](./Graphs%20Part%201.png)

EBIT increases from $10.7B in the downside case to $14.1B in the base case and $19.1B in the upside case. Net earnings also improve from $8.3B to $10.9B and $14.7B, showing stronger profitability as revenue growth improves and operating expenses become more favorable.

Costco’s EBIT margin improves from 3.03% in the downside case to 3.64% in the base case and 4.28% in the upside case. Net margin also rises from 2.35% to 2.80% and 3.29%, showing that even small improvements in cost structure can meaningfully improve profitability.

![Graphs Part 2](./Graphs%20Part%202.png)
